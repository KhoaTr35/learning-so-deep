Exploratory Analysis

Figure 1 — EDA overview. Classes are near-balanced (≈480–600 samples each). Word-count median ≈110, P95≈712 — a 192-token cap covers most samples. talk.* posts are shorter than comp.* / sci.*.

Primary Findings

  • Near-balanced classes: each topic holds ≈480–600 training samples, so no over-sampling was needed.
  • Heavy length tail: P95 word count reaches 712 words, but the median is only ≈110 — a fixed cap of 192 tokens covers the majority while staying GPU-safe.
  • Header noise: email metadata (From:, Subject:, Lines:) inflates token counts and shifts vocabulary; regex removal was applied before vocabulary construction.
  • URL / email leakage: raw corpus contains many email addresses and URLs that would act as spurious class signals without preprocessing.

Preprocessing Decisions

  • Lowercase + strip email headers with multi-pattern regex.
  • Remove URLs (http*) and email addresses (*@*).
  • Keep only [a-z0-9'\s] — punctuation is dropped.
  • Min-frequency threshold of 2 for vocabulary inclusion (removes rare typos / usernames).
  • CLS token prepended to every sequence for Transformer classification.
