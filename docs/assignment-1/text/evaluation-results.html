Key Findings

  • BiGRU outperforms Transformer by +8.25% accuracy despite having 40% fewer parameters — faster convergence wins within the given epoch budget.
  • Ensemble delivers marginal gain (+0.16% over BiGRU) with only 7,340 new parameters, showing that logit-level fusion can recover some Transformer diversity.
  • Hardest classes: talk.religion.misc (TF F1=0.07) and talk.politics.misc — heavily overlapping vocabulary with adjacent topics causes systematic confusion.
  • All models score AUC > 0.91 — calibrated probability estimates are robust even where hard-label accuracy is low.
  • The Transformer's slow convergence under label_smoothing=0.1 and 6 deep layers suggests it needs either more epochs or a pre-trained initialisation.

Next Experiment Steps

  • Pre-train embeddings on the full corpus (e.g., Word2Vec or GloVe) before fine-tuning the Transformer — this should close the gap with BiGRU.
  • Extend Transformer training to 25+ epochs or reduce dropout to 0.2 to surface its true capacity advantage.
  • Apply class-weighted loss for talk.religion.misc and talk.politics.misc to address the long tail of confusable low-recall classes.
  • Test temperature scaling on ensemble probability outputs to improve ROC AUC calibration.
